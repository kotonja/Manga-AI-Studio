# Manga AI Studio Tester Guide

Welcome to the Manga AI Studio private alpha. This is an early test version for a small group of invited testers.

## What It Is

Manga AI Studio helps turn a story idea into a draft manga project. It can create a story bible, characters, page plans, panel layouts, mock panel art, lettering, QA reports, and export files.

This alpha is mostly about testing the workflow. Some art is mock art, which means it is generated as placeholder manga-style test art so we can prove the pipeline works without paid image APIs.

## Logging In

You will receive a private tester token. Open the app link from the alpha operator, paste your token on the onboarding screen, and log in.

Do not share your token. Your token keeps your projects separate from other testers.

## Create A Demo Manga

1. Open the dashboard.
2. Click the demo or Founder Demo option.
3. Keep the default premise or edit it.
4. Click Generate Manga Demo.
5. Wait for the timeline to complete.
6. Click Open in Studio.

The default demo premise is:

> A lonely swordsman protects a ghost child in a ruined city.

## Open Director Mode

Director Mode lets you start from one premise and create a draft project.

1. Open a project.
2. Choose Director Mode from the project navigation.
3. Enter a short premise.
4. Pick genre, tone, page count, reading direction, quality mode, and provider.
5. Use mock provider unless the operator asks you to test real provider mode.
6. Generate the draft.

## Inspect Pages

Open Page Studio to see page thumbnails, panel rectangles, render status, bubbles, and QA markers. You can click pages and panels to inspect them.

## Edit Bubbles

Open Lettering Room or Page Studio, select a bubble, edit the text, and save. If text is too long, report it as unreadable text or confusing lettering.

## Run QA

Open QA Room for a project or page and run QA. QA checks for layout, missing renders, lettering issues, export readiness, and blocking problems.

## Export

Open Publishing Room, choose an export format, check readiness, and create the export. If an export is blocked, read the readiness checklist and report anything confusing.

## Report Bugs

Use the feedback button in the app when possible. Helpful reports include:

- What you were trying to do.
- What happened.
- What you expected.
- Project, page, or panel context if available.
- Whether the issue blocks you from continuing.

Use severity:

- Low: small confusion or visual polish.
- Medium: recoverable problem.
- High: a core workflow broke.
- Blocker: you cannot log in, generate, QA, export, or safely access your work.

## What Mock Art Means

Mock art is placeholder test art. It may look manga-inspired, but it is not final commercial art. Mock art proves the app pipeline works; it does not prove final art quality.

## What Not To Upload

Do not upload:

- Images you do not own or do not have permission to use.
- Copyrighted characters, franchise references, or style-copy requests.
- Private documents, IDs, addresses, or sensitive personal information.
- Anything you would not want alpha operators to see while debugging.

## Privacy Warning

This is a private alpha, not a finished consumer product. Operators may inspect project metadata, feedback, errors, and logs to fix bugs. Do not put highly sensitive personal material into alpha projects.
